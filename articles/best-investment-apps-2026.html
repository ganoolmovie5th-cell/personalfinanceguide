Best Investment Apps for Beginners 2026

You don't need thousands of dollars or a finance degree to start investing. With the right app, you can begin with just $5 and build wealth over time. The best investment apps for beginners combine low fees, easy interfaces, and educational resources to help you grow your money confidently.

This guide compares the top investment apps for beginners in 2026, breaking down their features, fees, and ideal users so you can choose the perfect platform for your financial journey.

💡 The Power of Starting Small: Investing $50/month at 8% annual returns grows to $74,000 in 30 years. The best time to start is now, and these apps make it incredibly easy.

What to Look for in an Investment App

Low or No Minimum Balance

As a beginner, you want to start small. Look for apps with $0 minimums so you can begin with whatever you can afford.

Low Fees

Fees eat into returns. Prioritize apps with:

  • $0 commission on stocks and ETFs
  • Low or no account fees
  • Low expense ratios on funds (under 0.20%)

Fractional Shares

Buy portions of expensive stocks. Instead of needing $500 for one Amazon share, invest $50 for 0.1 share.

Educational Resources

Beginner-friendly apps offer tutorials, articles, and tools to help you learn as you invest.

Automatic Investing

Set up recurring deposits and investments. Automation removes emotion and ensures consistency.

Top Investment Apps for Beginners 2026

1. Fidelity 🏆 Best Overall

Overview

Fidelity offers the perfect combination of beginner-friendly features and advanced tools you'll grow into. Zero fees, excellent research, and outstanding customer service.

Key Features:

  • $0 minimum balance
  • $0 commission on stocks, ETFs, and options
  • Fractional shares (starting at $1)
  • Excellent mobile and web platforms
  • 24/7 customer service
  • Comprehensive educational resources

Fees:

  • Account fee: $0
  • Stock/ETF trades: $0
  • Mutual funds: $0 for Fidelity funds, $49.95 for others
  • Expense ratios: 0.015% - 1.00%

Best For:

Beginners who want a full-featured broker that grows with them

2. Robinhood 🏆 Easiest to Use

Overview

Robinhood revolutionized investing with its simple, mobile-first approach. Perfect for true beginners who want a no-frills way to start.

Key Features:

  • $0 minimum balance
  • $0 commission on stocks, ETFs, and crypto
  • Fractional shares
  • Beautiful, intuitive mobile app
  • Cash management account with debit card

Fees:

  • Account fee: $0
  • Stock/ETF trades: $0
  • Options: $0 (but no advanced strategies)
  • Gold subscription: $5/month (optional, includes research and margin)

Best For:

True beginners who want the simplest possible experience

Cons:

  • Limited research tools
  • No mutual funds
  • Basic customer support

3. Betterment 🏆 Best Robo-Advisor

Overview

Betterment pioneered robo-advising—investing made completely automatic. Perfect for hands-off beginners who want professional portfolio management.

Key Features:

  • $0 minimum balance
  • Automated portfolio management
  • Goal-based investing
  • Tax-loss harvesting
  • Socially responsible investing options
  • Cash Reserve (high-yield savings)

Fees:

  • Digital plan: 0.25% annual fee ($4/month for $20,000)
  • Premium plan: 0.40% (includes human advisors, $100k minimum)
  • Fund expense ratios: ~0.05-0.15%

Best For:

Hands-off investors who want automatic professional management

4. Acorns 🏆 Best for Spare Change Investing

Overview

Acorns rounds up your purchases and invests the spare change. The easiest way to start investing without thinking about it.

Key Features:

  • Round-up investing (spare change from purchases)
  • $5 minimum to start
  • Automated recurring investments
  • Found Money (cashback from partner brands)
  • Retirement accounts (IRA)
  • Banking features (checking, debit card)

Fees:

  • Personal: $3/month
  • Family: $5/month (includes kids' investment accounts)

Best For:

People who struggle to save, want completely passive investing

Note:

Monthly fee is high for small balances. $36/year on $500 = 7.2% fee. Better for balances $2,000+.

5. Webull 🏆 Best for Active Beginners

Overview

Webull offers more advanced features than Robinhood while remaining beginner-friendly. Great for beginners who want to learn technical analysis.

Key Features:

  • $0 minimum balance
  • $0 commission on stocks, ETFs, options
  • Fractional shares
  • Advanced charting tools
  • Paper trading (practice with fake money)
  • Extended hours trading

Fees:

  • Account fee: $0
  • Stock/ETF trades: $0
  • Options: $0
  • Margin: Variable rates

Best For:

Beginners interested in learning technical analysis and active trading

6. M1 Finance 🏆 Best for Custom Portfolios

Overview

M1 combines the automation of robo-advisors with the control of self-directed investing. Create custom "pies" of stocks and ETFs.

Key Features:

  • $100 minimum ($500 for retirement accounts)
  • Custom portfolio "pies"
  • Automatic rebalancing
  • Fractional shares
  • Smart transfers (cash management)
  • Margin loans (M1 Plus)

Fees:

  • Basic: $0
  • M1 Plus: $3/month (includes cashback, lower margin rates)

Best For:

Investors who want automation but also control over their portfolio

7. Vanguard 🏆 Best for Long-Term Investors

Overview

Vanguard is the king of low-cost index funds. Owned by fund shareholders, so profits go back to investors through lower fees.

Key Features:

  • $0 minimum for ETFs
  • $1,000-$3,000 minimum for mutual funds
  • Lowest expense ratios in the industry
  • Excellent target-date funds
  • Comprehensive research

Fees:

  • Account fee: $0
  • Stock/ETF trades: $0
  • Mutual funds: $0 for Vanguard funds
  • Expense ratios: 0.03% - 0.40% (industry lowest)

Best For:

Long-term investors focused on low-cost index funds

Cons:

  • Higher minimums for mutual funds
  • Less intuitive platform
  • No fractional shares for stocks

How to Choose Your First Investment App

Step 1: Determine Your Investing Style

  • Hands-off: Betterment, Acorns, Vanguard target-date funds
  • Hands-on: Fidelity, Robinhood, Webull, M1 Finance
  • Spare change: Acorns
  • Custom portfolios: M1 Finance

Step 2: Consider Your Starting Amount

  • Under $100: Robinhood, Fidelity, Betterment
  • $100-$500: Any app except Vanguard mutual funds
  • $500+: Any app, including Acorns (fee becomes reasonable)

Step 3: Think About Your Goals

  • Retirement: Betterment, Vanguard, Fidelity
  • General wealth building: Any app
  • Learning to invest: Robinhood, Webull, Fidelity
  • Completely passive: Betterment, Acorns

Getting Started: Your First Week

🎯 Day 1: Download and Setup
  • Download your chosen app
  • Complete account setup and verification
  • Link your bank account
🎯 Day 2-3: Fund Your Account
  • Transfer initial deposit ($50-$500)
  • Set up automatic recurring deposits
  • Even $25/week adds up to $1,300/year
🎯 Day 4-5: Make Your First Investment
  • For robo-advisors: Complete risk questionnaire
  • For self-directed: Research one ETF (VTI, VOO, or similar)
  • Buy your first investment
🎯 Day 6-7: Learn and Plan
  • Explore app's educational resources
  • Set up automatic investing
  • Schedule monthly check-ins

Common Beginner Mistakes to Avoid

❌ Trying to Pick Individual Stocks

As a beginner, stick with diversified ETFs or let robo-advisors handle it. Individual stock picking requires research most beginners don't have time for.

❌ Investing Money You Might Need Soon

Only invest money you won't need for 5+ years. Short-term money belongs in savings accounts.

❌ Checking Your Portfolio Daily

Markets fluctuate daily. Checking constantly leads to emotional decisions. Check monthly or quarterly.

❌ Not Diversifying

Don't put all money in one stock or sector. Total market ETFs (like VTI) provide instant diversification.

❌ Paying High Fees

A 1% fee difference over 30 years can cost tens of thousands. Prioritize low-cost options.

The Bottom Line

The best investment app is the one that gets you started. Don't overthink it—every day you delay is a day of potential compound growth lost.

Start with Fidelity for the most well-rounded experience, Robinhood for simplicity, or Betterment for complete automation. Open your account this week, fund it with whatever you can afford, and make your first investment.

Remember: The perfect app doesn't exist, but the perfect time to start does—it's right now.
